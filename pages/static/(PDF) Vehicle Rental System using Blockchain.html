ABSTRACT
A blockchain is a decentralized, distributed, and often times public, digital ledger
consisting of records called blocks that are used to record transactions across many
computers so that any involved block can not be involved retroactively. A blockchain
database is managed automatically using peer to peer system and a distributing time
stamping server. The competition in the vehicle rental industry is growing very fast. The
companies are trying to improve the system by using innovative methods. A long-time
rental of vehicles along with self-driving is proposed in this paper where security and
safety of the vehicles are always a matter of concern. So Blockchain is introduced to
remove this dilemma. In this paper a detailed integration of Blockchain and vehicle rental
system is introduced and how it can be so great for both car renters and car owners. A
higher security, safety as well as a great authentication is shown throughout this course so
that rental industries can run their business optimally.
Keywords: Vehicle rental, Security, Renters, Owners, Proof-of-stake, Blockchain,
Cryptocurrency, Hyperledger Fabric, Smart contracts

Introduction
1.1 Overview
A vehicle rental industry rents out vehicles to its consumers who want any type of
rented car for some time for their personal usage. Sometime the consumers want the
rented cars for short period of time and sometime for long period of time. The German
company Sixt is the first known car rental service provider. Travel increased a lot which
led to a continuation of car rental businesses after world war 2. Sells of Cars has
significantly increased in the 20th century, but the aptitude seems to be decreasing for
many factors such as traffic, pollution, better public transport, etc. That is why when
people need a vehicle they choose to rent it rather than own it. In the time of digital era,
these rental ministries have been extended with technology. Now consumers can look at
different options on websites or mobile applications. Tech- nology has been a blessing in
this business as owners now can track their vehicles with the help of Global Positioning
Systems(GPS). The Internet has enhanced the reach of such businesses additionally.
Despite having all these advantages, there remains many issues such as
centralization,absence of trust and so on. This problem also can be solved by getting
proof of location with the help of peer-to-peer networks [1]. This helps the owners to get
the geographical position of the consumers. And with the help of this network owners can
relief from the charges of the middlemen.With the help of blockchain and smart contracts,
this middlemen issue can be solved. We will show you how this problem can be solved
through this paper.
2
1.2 Limitations of existing system
Low transparency - In this case transparency refers to everything being open to
the public, not only the source code but also the responsible, documents, reports of people
etc.
Maximum expense - There is a lot of sharing businesses that have flourished in
recent times but these businesses are of high costs as the industries tend to take a higher
commission. This puts real pressure both on the true owners and users of the service.
These businesses do not have any assets, they just act as intermediaries taking a big
chunk of profits from the owners.
Lack of trust - The absence of trust is the main issue in the rental business. Pro-
prietors expect from the leaseholders to pay their sums as soon as possible but they
neglect to keep their pledge when these deposits are made. On the other hand, the
leaseholders misuse the vehicle and do not agree to pay the appropriate bills.
1.3 Problem Statement and Motivation
The basic structure of this project is the blockchain which is a growing list of records
called blocks, which are linked using cryptography. The blocks in our blockchain will
give feedback on the transaction made when any vehicle is rented. Each block will have
data stored in the form of Merkle trees. It is a basic structure of a tree in which each leaf
node is a segment of a block of data, and each non-leaf node is a segment of its children.

1.4 Objective
Consumers can rent vehicles according to their needs. They can rent any type of
vehicle with or without drivers. This may lead some time to mistrust as many car rental
industries reported about their missing vehicles. That is why blockchain is used in this
method to get the geographical location using peer to peer network. Specific aims of this
thesis that should be mentioned:
a. To reduce the general people’s sufferings by providing them every type of
vehicle flexibilities.
b. To validate the Rental Vehicle system by using a user satisfaction test.
c. To transform the manual process of hiring cars into a computerized system to
ensure the security of every vehicle.
d, To give maximum security to both users and owners.
e. To reduce the extra cost that an owner has to bear for the middlemen.

4.2 Blockchain Working Procedure
A blockchain is a decentralized, distributed, and oftentimes digital ledger in public
that consists of records called blocks which are used to record transactions across many
computers so that any involved block in the transaction cannot be altered robustly,
without the alteration of all subsequent blocks. The blocks hold combinations of valid
transactions that are hashed and encoded into single Merkle tree. Each block in the
transaction includes the cryptographic hash of the prior block in the blockchain, which
links the two blocks. The blocks that are linked form a chain. The integrity of the
previous block is confirmed by this iterative process, all the way back to the initial block,
which is known as the genesis block. A Merkle tree is a tree where every leaf node is
labeled with the cryptographic hash of a data block. Moreover, every non-leaf node is
labeled with the cryptographic hash of the labels of its child nodes. Merkle tree is also
known as Hash tree. Hash lists and hash chains combines to a hash tree. In this project,
the transaction made during the renting of the vehicle will be represented by the blocks in
our blockchain. Each block will store data in the structure of Merkle trees. Merkle tree is
a special tree structure in which a block of data is hashed in each leaf node and each
non-leaf node is a hash of its children. There are up to 2 children on each node in a
Merkle tree. This technique used in the Merkle tree provides a security measurement
against undesirable alteration of data by hackers. The mentioned technique works with
information broken down into pieces of 512 bits in total. Then this technique generates its
cryptographic by ”mixing” and then reveals a hash code of 256 bits. The algorithm
consists of a very simple procedure and it is repeated 64 times. All transactions in this
technique will take place in a network that is permissionless and where the miner
transactions will be paid to an originator. These originators will be in the structure of
transaction fees during each transaction on the network. The transaction fees will be very
less compared to other vehicle rental platforms. We will use Proof of Work to achieve
consistency. A Proof of Work (PoW) system is an economic measure to resist refusal of
service attacks and other service misuses such as spam on a network by requiring some
work from the service requester.

Particularly, vehicles may collude to defraud the advertiser to obtain rewards without disseminating ads, which may cause unfair “free-riding” issue in these activities. Furthermore, concerns on possible privacy leakage may discourage vehicles to participate in the process of ad dissemination.
